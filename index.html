2023 · 2024

UI/UX Product Designer

Plunet.com / SaaS

Redesign of the Test System Manager · a centralized tool to manage BM instances, deploy data snapshots, and monitor services. Structured around weekly CoP meetings, dark-mode interface using the PlunetBoard design system.

Figma · Mural · Jira · Confluence

Context

The Test System Manager (TSM) originated from the need to streamline how internal teams managed test environments. Until then, system administrators and dev teams relied on fragmented interfaces to configure instances, deploy data snapshots, and monitor diagnostics. The previous tool lacked usability, was hard to scale, and failed to surface critical functionality efficiently — especially for non-expert users.

Goal

Redesign the TSM control panel as a centralized, intuitive tool to manage BusinessManager (BM) instances, streamline configurations, deploy data snapshots, and monitor services — while ensuring extensibility for future features. Improve usability, reduce time spent on repetitive admin tasks, and support both technical and non-technical users.

Process

An iterative, collaborative redesign structured around weekly Community of Practice meetings. Key pain points emerged from sessions with developers, QA engineers, and system administrators — revealing the need for better log visibility, faster snapshot deployment, and a clearer overview of BM instances.

The information architecture was restructured to reflect users' mental models, with a dark-mode interface built on the PlunetBoard design system — reusing its component library to ensure consistency and optimize resources. Interactive Figma prototypes validated critical flows like system configuration, service control, and log viewing.

Outcome

Successfully adopted by development and testing teams as the primary tool for managing test systems. Feedback highlighted faster task completion, clearer visibility of critical information, and a smoother workflow. The modular design also enabled rapid incorporation of new features post-launch.

UI/UX Design Intern

Green-Coding.io / SaaS

Redesign of a platform that measures energy consumption and CO₂ emissions from software and websites. Black and white palette inspired by a console · clarity, accessibility, and a deliberate distance from conventional green eco-aesthetics. Delivered a functional, static, minimalist MVP.

Figma · Google Forms · GitHub

Context

Green Coding is a platform that measures energy consumption and CO₂ emissions from software and websites. The goal was to redesign the homepage, add functionalities like project comparison, and create a "Showcases" section · all while prioritizing clarity in the presentation of complex data.

Team & Tools

Role: UI/UX Design Intern

Figma · Google Forms · GitHub

Phase 01

Research

Stakeholder interviews and user surveys revealed that 96% of users were interested in reducing their digital footprint but unsure how. In-depth exploration of digital energy consumption and sustainable design limitations informed the direction.

Phase 02

Definition

Reorganized the information architecture to promote ethical and ecological navigation. Defined the primary User Persona: Joao, a developer concerned about his environmental impact who needed a tool that was clear, fast, and actionable without greenwashing aesthetics.

Phase 03

Ideation & Design

Black and white palette · chosen for clarity, accessibility, and to stand apart from the clichéd green palette common in eco-design. Visual metaphor inspired by a developer console: clear hierarchy, low cognitive load, and a scalable structure that communicates precision rather than decoration.

Result

A functional, static, minimalist, and accessible MVP that allows users to:

↔Compare projects visually and simply
